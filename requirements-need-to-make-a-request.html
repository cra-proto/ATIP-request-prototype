What you will need to make a request

If you can’t find the information you are looking for at Find information already available , make a formal request through the Access to Information Act or Privacy Act. Please note this process may take at least 30 days and there is a $5 fee for requests under the Access to Information Act, but there is no fee for requests for personal information under the Privacy Act.

New process for representatives requesting income statements from the last 3 years, notices of assessment, and benefits information

Effective September 10, 2024, the CRA is updating its process. The ATIP Directorate will no longer process your requests for your client’s income statements from the last 3 years, notices of assessment, and benefits information. Your client can get this information through My Account or other CRA services. For more information about these changes, go to Changes to access to information and privacy requests at the Canada Revenue Agency.

Are you eligible?

To make a request, you must be one of the following:

  • a Canadian citizen
  • a permanent resident of Canada
  • an individual or corporation currently in Canada
  • individuals outside of Canada (under the Privacy Act only)

Requirements for submitting an ATIP request

To submit an Access to Information and Privacy (ATIP) request, the necessary documentation will vary based on the type of request and whether you are requesting information for yourself or on behalf of someone else. Please use the drop-down menus provided to select your request type and choose the description that best reflects your situation to learn what you need to provide for the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) to process your request.

Access your business information

GO to My Business Account for your:

  • Tax returns
  • Corporate account balance
  • Account transactions
  • notices of assessment or reassessment

For a complete list of information you can get through My Business Account, go to What can I do on my Business Account. Or call the business enquiries line at 1-800-959-5525 to request corporate income tax documents.

CRA's information holdings

Many of the records held by the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) are readily accessible to the public, this includes:

  • Policy manuals
  • Financial records
  • Account transactions
  • Information about government operations

Visit the Info Source and the Reading Room Services to explore available information.

Access information about registered charities

Accessing information about registered charities and other qualified donee does not require a formal request. Here are ways you can obtain this information quickly and at no cost:

  • Online list of charities and other qualified donee:

    The CRA maintains a list of charities and other qualified donees. This searchable database allows you to view information about each organization, including their status and publicly available financial documents.

  • Informal requests for charity information:

    If you need more information about a particular charity or other qualified donees, you can make an informal request. This is often faster and more direct than going through a formal request. For guidance on how to make an informal request, go to Request registered charity information.

Go to How to get information about a charityfor more information.
